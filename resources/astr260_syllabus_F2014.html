Course Description:

ASTR 260 (3 credit) will offer an introduction to unix command language and programming skills relevant to Astronomy. Algorithms will be presented and students will be programming in either C, Fortran 77, Fortran 90 or Python, whichever they have more experience. Some special sessions of Python, IDL, and IRAF will be covered. Problems will come from physics and astrophysics situations however most of the efforts by the students will be in the astrophysics problems via numerical methods and, of course, in debugging.

This course draws heavily from MATH 205, MATH 206, PHYS 170, PHYS 171, and CS 150; some material in the context of ASTR 180, ASTR 181, and ASTR 250 will be discussed. 

Grading Policy:

The course grade will be based on:

  • Bi-weekly problem sets (40% of grade)
  • Two Mid-term exams (each 40% of grade)
  • Final exam (20% of grade)

The grading scale adopted will be:

  • 90-100% - A
  • 80-89% - B
  • 70-79% - C
  • 60-69% - D
  • 0 - 59% - F

Problem sets will be announced in class and made available on Laulima (https://laulima.hawaii.edu). Assignments are due at the beginning of the lecture the day they are due. It is the responsibility of each student to find out if there are assignments and when they are due especially when they are absent during lectures. Late submissions will be penalized in that for each day the assignment is turned late, 10% of its score will be deducted. For example, if an assignment is turned in 3 days late, the maximum score attained for a perfect assignment is 70%. There is a limit though in that assignments turned in late more than but equal to 7 days will not be accepted. Homework can be turned in in person or electronically at the beginning of the class of the day it is due. Working in groups is highly encouraged but the final assignment must be individual effort.

IMPORTANT: Problems sets must be submitted through laulima. Use the Drop Box menu on the left tab to submit your work. Any problems set submitted via email will absolutely not be accepted.

Exams will measure the students' understanding of the material covered in class and will reflect the effort invested in problem solving. Thus exams will measure the student's understanding of the concepts encountered in the assignments. No exam problem will be exactly the same as a problem set. Exams will usually consist of quantitative and qualitative problems. Exams must be taken on the date and time when they are scheduled. No make-up tests will be given except under extreme circumstances such as a medical emergency. If you know in advance that you will not be able to take the exam on the pre-assigned dates, please talk to the instructor at least one week before the exam is scheduled to arrange a new time. Exams will be take home. Students can consult any book, class notes, homework, and textbooks but they must turn in work done completely on his/her own. UHH abides to the student code of conduct. Any suspicion that students copied will result in a zero mark on that assignment without a chance to retake it.
